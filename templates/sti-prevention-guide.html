Overview

Sexually transmitted infections (STIs) are infections that are spread through sexual contact. This means they can be passed from one person to another during vaginal, anal, or oral sex. STIs are caused by different types of germs, including bacteria, viruses, or parasites. Some of these infections can be easily treated with medicine, while others, like viruses, can stay in the body for life. If STIs are not treated, they can lead to serious health problems. For instance, some STIs can cause pain, discomfort, and other issues like infertility, which means difficulty having children. Others, such as HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus), can weaken the immune system.

Prevention Methods

There are several ways to prevent STIs. It's important to practice safe sex, communicate with your partner, and take preventive measures. Here are some effective prevention methods:

Condom Use

Using condoms correctly every time you have sex significantly reduces the risk of getting or spreading most STIs.

Vaccination

Vaccines are available for certain STIs like HPV (Human Papillomavirus) and Hepatitis B. Getting vaccinated is an effective preventive measure.

Regular Testing

Getting regularly tested for STIs helps detect any infections early and allows for timely treatment and management.

Monogamy

Having sex with a mutually exclusive partner (monogamous relationship) reduces the chances of STI transmission.

Symptoms of STIs

While some STIs may cause noticeable symptoms, others may not. It's important to be aware of common symptoms, including:

  • Unusual discharge from the genitals
  • Pain or discomfort during urination
  • Itching, irritation, or sores around the genitals
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Pain during sex

If you experience any of these symptoms, seek medical attention promptly for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Risk Factors

Certain behaviors and circumstances can increase the likelihood of getting an STI. These include:

  • Multiple sexual partners
  • Unprotected sex
  • Drug use or alcohol use that impairs judgment
  • Having a history of STIs
