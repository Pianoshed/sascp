Hepatitis Care

Screening, diagnosis, and management of viral hepatitis to ensure healthy liver outcomes.

What is Hepatitis?

Hepatitis refers to inflammation of the liver, often caused by viral infections. Hepatitis can result in liver damage, cirrhosis, or liver cancer if left untreated. It is critical to diagnose and manage hepatitis early to ensure healthy liver outcomes.

Types of Hepatitis

There are several types of hepatitis viruses, and each type affects the liver differently. The five main types of hepatitis are:

  • Hepatitis A (HAV): Spread through contaminated food and water, usually resolves on its own with no lasting damage.
  • Hepatitis B (HBV): Spread through contact with infected blood or bodily fluids. Can lead to chronic infection, cirrhosis, and liver cancer.
  • Hepatitis C (HCV): Primarily spread through blood contact. Most people with HCV develop chronic liver disease.
  • Hepatitis D (HDV): Only occurs in people infected with hepatitis B. It worsens the progression of HBV infection.
  • Hepatitis E (HEV): Spread mainly through contaminated water. It usually resolves on its own but can be serious in pregnant women.

Screening & Diagnosis

Hepatitis can be diagnosed through blood tests that detect viral infections or liver damage. Regular screening for high-risk populations is essential to catch the disease early.

Prevention

Prevention strategies include:

  • Vaccination for hepatitis A and B
  • Safe practices in healthcare settings
  • Avoiding sharing needles and other personal items
  • Good hygiene and sanitation practices
